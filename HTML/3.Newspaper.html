Motorola Set To Acquire Cable Maker

16 September 1999

Motorola Inc. confirmed today that it would acquire the General Instrument Corporation, a leading maker
of cable set-top boxes, in a stock deal valued at nearly $11 billion.

The move would make Motorola a key supplier of cable television equipment and allow the company to
develop what it calls a ''home hub'' of products to offer broadband cable and Internet access to homes.
